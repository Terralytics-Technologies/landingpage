The Climate Risk Decision Gap

Climate risk data is becoming more available, but decision-making remains fragmented.

Flood maps, hazard layers, and model outputs do not translate directly into the questions organizations actually face: Where are we exposed? What are the financial implications? What actions reduce risk?

Across government and financial sectors, institutions are being asked to make decisions with tools that were never designed for operational or financial use.

The result is a growing gap between available data and actionable intelligence.

Data Inputs

Flood maps, hazard layers, infrastructure records, portfolio data, and model outputs.

Decision Gap

Available datasets rarely map cleanly to operational, investment, and financial questions.

Decision Intelligence

Actionable risk translation for prioritization, investment planning, and resilience strategy.
